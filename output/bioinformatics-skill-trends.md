## What is trending
- Python is the true baseline. Every role in this sample asks for it explicitly or assumes it in core bioinformatics work.
- Pipelines are now central, not optional. Four out of five roles mention workflow building, automation, or production processing.
- The market is split between discovery science and production engineering. You can see that in the mix of computational biologist roles on one side and workflow-heavy engineer roles on the other.
- Oncology still dominates the commercial hiring signal here. More than half the roles are tied to cancer diagnostics, liquid biopsy, biomarkers, or oncology assay development.
- Cloud and ML are no longer niche add-ons. They appear in just over half of the roles, especially in startups and platform teams.
- Collaboration is a hiring requirement, not soft garnish. Every posting expects the person to work directly with wet-lab, assay, software, product, translational, or business teams.

## Practical skills index for a candidate roadmap
### Tier 1: highest current demand
- Python
- Workflow and pipeline development
- Statistical analysis of genomics data
- Cross-functional communication
- Core NGS and genomics analysis

### Tier 2: strong differentiators
- R for analysis and plotting
- Cloud-native execution
- ML or applied modeling
- Experimental design with assay or wet-lab teams
- Software engineering practices for reproducible bioinformatics

### Tier 3: role-specific accelerators
- Oncology and liquid biopsy experience
- Single-cell and perturbation analysis
- Workflow engines like Nextflow, WDL, or Snakemake
- Clinical biomarker strategy
- Drug discovery and multi-omics integration
